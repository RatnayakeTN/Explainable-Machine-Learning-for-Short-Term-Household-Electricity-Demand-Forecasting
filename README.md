Accurate short-term forecasting of residential level is vital for energy management in countries such as Sri Lanka, where the growing consumption has continued to challenge the adaptability of the energy sector. This study develops and evaluates the use of tree-based ensemble models of Random Forest, Extreme Gradient Boosting, Light Gradient Boosting and Categorical Gradient Boosting for forecasting household electricity consumption using smart meter data from Residential Electricity Consumption Dataset for Sri Lanka (RECON-SL), where a subset of the dataset that contains records of 1438 smart meter data in 6-hour intervals was utilised.
	
The dataset consisted of significant challenges due to irregular sampling intervals and high variability across different households. To address these challenges, a comprehensive data processing pipeline was implemented where missing values were handled using interpolation techniques and the data was resampled to have consistent time intervals. Feature engineering played a vital role, by incorporating time-based features such as hour of day, day of week, lag features and rolling statistics to capture the temporal tendencies. Additionally household level normalization was applied to reduce the variability in the scale of consumption and to improve model generalization.
	
A time series cross validation approach was used to ensure proper temporal evaluation and reduce and avoid data leakage. Model performance was assessed using multiple metrics, including Mean Squared Error (MSE), Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE), Mean Absolute Error (MAE), Symmetric Mean Absolute Percentage Error (sMAPE), and R-squared (R²). 
	
The results indicate that the Random Forest model achieved the best overall performance in terms of RMSE, MAE, and R², demonstrating strong capability in capturing general consumption patterns. In contrast, XGBoost produced the lowest sMAPE, indicating better performance in handling proportional errors, particularly for low consumption values. Overall, the study highlights the importance of robust preprocessing, feature engineering, and appropriate evaluation techniques in time series forecasting. The findings provide valuable insights for improving household-level energy prediction and support future research in scalable and reliable smart grid analytics.
